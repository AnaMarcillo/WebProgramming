Conferences, presentations and exhibitor

  • Speaker of the scientific article "Reviewing Inpainting Techniques Using Diffusion Models: A Comprehensive Analysis and Evaluation" at INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE ON APPLIED ENGINEERING AND INNOVATIVE TECHNOLOGIES (AENIT-2023)

    The main purpose of this study was to provide a complete and critical view of the effectiveness of these techniques in image restoration, highlighting their application and performance in various contexts.

  • Speaker of scientific poster: "A Spatial-Temporal Stochastic Model for Forecasting Epilepsy Seizures" at METMA-LATAM (Modelización Estadística Espacio Temporal - América Latina)

    We presented a spatial-temporal model for detecting and forecasting epileptic seizures using signal-processing techniques, the SARIMA model, and Kalman Filters. We analyze EEG signals from patients experiencing different types of epileptic convulsions (WIAS, IAS, and FBTC) using the Siena Scalp database. In particular, a patient with IAS seizure. Our main objective was forecasting and preventing seizures through spatio-temporal data analysis and creating heat maps to identify high cerebral activity.

  • Author of "Creation of an Alert Device for Early Detection of Epilepsy Using an EEG Signal Power Threshold" presented at TICEC - 2023

    We published a study focused on the analysis of specific patterns in the EEG signals of patients with epilepsy, extracted from the Siena Scalp database. Signal processing techniques such as baseband filters and the wavelet transform method were used to identify predictive indicators of epileptic seizures. The effectiveness of visual analysis of preictal phases and electronic devices for seizure detection and prediction was discussed.

    DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-45438-7_20

  • Author of "Topological Analysis Techniques for Improving Neural Network Performance in COVID-19 Detection Using Persistent Homology" at V International Congress of Computer Science, Electronics, and Industrial Engineering

    We developed a study about the application of topological data analysis techniques in neural networks used in the detection of COVID-19, with the aim of improving their predictive capacity. Using homological persistence, a mathematical tool for extracting topological features from complex data sets, we transformed chest x-ray images into a representation of these topological features.
